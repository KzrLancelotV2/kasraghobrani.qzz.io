About Me

I am a Computer Engineering undergraduate at the University of Tehran with a passion for artificial intelligence, machine learning, and complex systems. What began as an interest in modern AI gradually expanded into network science, graph learning, and more recently computational biology, where I became fascinated by how machine learning can be used to understand living systems.

Born in December 2003, I graduated from Mofid High School before starting my studies at the University of Tehran. Throughout my academic journey, I have been drawn to interdisciplinary problems that connect ideas from different fields and require a combination of mathematical thinking, computational methods, and scientific curiosity.

Outside of research, I enjoy traveling, learning languages, reading about history and politics, and maintaining a regular fitness routine. I am particularly interested in understanding how complex systems emerge and evolve, whether they are biological networks, social systems, economies, or human societies.

Today, my primary research interests lie in AI for Life Sciences, Gene Regulatory Networks, Foundation Models for Biology, and the broader goal of using machine learning to better understand complex biological processes.
