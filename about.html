About Me

I am a Computer Engineering undergraduate at the University of Tehran. Born in December 2003, I graduated from Mofid High School before beginning my studies in computer engineering.

I have always been fascinated by complex systems and the ways in which simple interactions can give rise to surprisingly sophisticated behavior. This curiosity extends far beyond technology and influences many of the subjects I enjoy learning about.

Outside of academics, I enjoy traveling, learning languages, reading about history and politics, and staying physically active through regular exercise. I enjoy exploring new cultures and perspectives, and I am particularly interested in understanding how societies, economies, and historical events evolve over time.

Whether through research, travel, books, or conversations, I am always looking for opportunities to learn something new and broaden my understanding of the world.
